Prenuptial Agreements in 2026: Are They Still Worth It?

June, 2026 By Em nuel

Once viewed as a tool reserved for the ultra-wealthy or a sign of marital distrust, the prenuptial agreement has undergone a dramatic transformation. In 2026, prenups are not only mainstream—they're increasingly viewed as a practical, even romantic, tool for building a transparent and secure future together .

Today's couples are marrying later, often bringing significant assets, businesses, and even digital wealth into the marriage. They're also more pragmatic. A recent YouGov survey found that 42% of women aged 18-24 would be likely to sign a prenup, compared to 36% of men, signaling a generational shift toward financial clarity . With new state laws, evolving case law, and the rise of cryptocurrency and creator economies, the question "Are prenups still worth it?" has a resounding answer: more than ever. This guide explores why.

Beyond Divorce: Prenups as Marriage Planning Tools

"When you hear the word prenup, it may sound like planning for divorce. In reality, a prenuptial agreement is about planning your marriage. It allows you and your partner to decide how finances, property, and responsibilities will be handled during your marriage based on your priorities and goals" .

The process of creating a prenup forces couples to have essential conversations about money, debt, savings habits, family expectations, and future goals—topics that many couples avoid. These discussions can help both partners start their marriage with shared understanding and fewer unanswered questions .

South Carolina: Court-Approved Prenups

H. 4800 - Introduced January 13, 2026, currently in House Judiciary Committee

This bill would allow family courts to approve prenuptial and postnuptial agreements, creating a rebuttable presumption of validity for court-approved agreements .

West Virginia: Uniform Premarital Agreement Act

West Virginia Code §48-1A-101 et seq.

"A premarital agreement is not enforceable if the party against whom enforcement is sought proves that: (1) That party did not execute the agreement voluntarily; or (2) The agreement was unconscionable when it was executed and, before execution, that party was not provided adequate financial disclosures and did not waive disclosure" .

Key provisions under the Uniform Act :

  • ✓ Must be in writing and signed by both parties
  • ✓ Enforceable without consideration
  • ✓ May modify or eliminate spousal support
  • ✓ Cannot adversely affect child support rights
  • ✓ Domestic violence remedies cannot be limited
  • ✓ Grounds for divorce cannot be modified
  • ✓ Custody provisions not binding on court

The Act applies to premarital agreements signed on or after July 1, 2023 .

Virginia: Premarital Agreement Act

Virginia Code § 20-147 through 20-155

"Parties to a premarital agreement may contract with respect to: (1) The rights and obligations of each of the parties in any of the property of either or both of them whenever and wherever acquired or located; (2) The right to buy, sell, use, transfer, exchange, abandon, lease, consume, expend, assign... (3) The disposition of property upon separation, marital dissolution, death... (4) Spousal support..."

Virginia's statute specifically provides that recitations in the agreement create a prima facie presumption that they are factually correct .

International Perspective: England and Wales in 2026

In England and Wales, prenuptial agreements are not automatically legally binding by statute, but courts will uphold them in most circumstances if certain conditions are met .

"The court should give effect to a nuptial agreement that is freely entered into by each party with a full appreciation of its implications, unless in the circumstances prevailing it would not be fair to hold the parties to their agreement."
— Radmacher v Granatino (2010)

LGBT+ Couples: Special Considerations in 2026

Cameron Jack of Giambrone & Partners notes: "Many LGBT+ couples we advise have been together for decades, cohabiting long before legal marriage or civil partnership was an option. A prenuptial agreement allows couples to define which assets were acquired during the long-term cohabitation phase versus the legal marriage phase, and ensure that if children are involved—whether through surrogacy, adoption, or donor arrangements—their financial security and inheritance rights are explicitly ring-fenced" .

The Prenup Process: Timeline to "I Do"

1
Initial Consultation (6+ months out): Meet with specialist family lawyer to discuss objectives, assets, and concerns .
2
Full Financial Disclosure: Both parties provide complete information about assets, income, debts, pensions, and any other relevant financial matters .
3
Drafting & Negotiation: Solicitor drafts agreement; both parties through independent lawyers negotiate terms .
4
Independent Legal Advice (at least 28 days before wedding): Each party receives advice from own solicitor; agreement finalized .
5
Signing: Executed at least 21-28 days before marriage to avoid pressure concerns .
6
Review & Update: Consider reviewing every 5 years or after major life events (birth of children, significant financial changes) .

What If You're Already Married? Postnuptial Agreements

If you didn't create a prenup before marriage, a postnuptial agreement serves the same purpose but is created after the wedding .

In Virginia, married persons may enter into agreements "to the same extent, with the same effect, and subject to the same conditions, as for agreements between prospective spouses" .

West Virginia law similarly allows amendment or revocation only by written agreement signed by both parties .

Frequently Asked Questions in 2026

Are prenups only for the wealthy?

No. "Prenups are used by couples with many different financial situations, especially when family assets, businesses, or future plans are involved" .

How much does a prenup cost?

Costs vary based on complexity. In the UK, firms like Hatch Brenner offer initial fixed-fee meetings (£150 for an hour with a specialist) . In the US, costs typically range from $1,500-$5,000 for simpler agreements, more for complex assets.

How long does a prenup last?

Generally, a prenup remains in effect throughout the marriage unless amended or revoked. However, courts may consider changed circumstances in long marriages . Many lawyers recommend reviewing every five years or after significant life events .

Can a prenup be challenged?

Yes, if it was not voluntarily executed, if disclosure was inadequate, if it was unconscionable at signing, or if it leaves one party in need .

The Verdict: Are Prenups Still Worth It in 2026?

Yes—and for more reasons than ever.

  • Financial transparency: The process fosters honest conversations about money, debt, and goals .
  • Asset protection: From cryptocurrency to family businesses, prenups safeguard what you've built .
  • Blended family security: Ensure children from previous relationships are protected .
  • Certainty and clarity: Avoid costly, emotional disputes if the marriage ends .
  • State-law advantages: New 2026 legislation in states like South Carolina may offer enhanced enforceability .

As attitudes have shifted, prenuptial agreements are no longer limited to one narrow scenario. They are now used by couples with a wide range of backgrounds, incomes, and family structures who want to protect what they have built and plan thoughtfully for the future .

Whether you're a Gen Z couple with crypto assets, a business owner entering a second marriage, or simply want to start your marriage with complete transparency, a prenuptial agreement in 2026 offers peace of mind that default state laws simply cannot provide.
